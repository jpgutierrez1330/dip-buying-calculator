Price Z-Score Tier 2 · Mean Reversion
What it measures: Standard deviations below 20-day average. Critically: At 3–6 month horizons, this signal FIGHTS intermediate momentum (Jegadeesh & Titman 1993). At 1+ year horizons it's a strong contrarian signal (DeBondt & Thaler 1985). Weight varies dramatically by holding period.
Easiest method: Bollinger Bands on TradingView — price below the lower band (20-period, 2-StdDev) ≈ Z-score ≤ −2.0. For exact value: Thinkorswim has built-in "PriceZScore" study.

Insider Buying — Cluster Purchases Signal 9 · Fundamental
What it measures: Whether company officers, directors, or 10%+ shareholders are making open-market purchases of their own stock using personal funds. This is the only fundamental signal in this calculator — all others measure price action or market sentiment. Insiders possess private information about future earnings, operations, and value. When multiple insiders buy simultaneously (cluster buying), their combined conviction is roughly 2× as predictive as a single purchase. Lakonishok & Lee (2001, RFS): strongest-buy portfolios earned 4.8% excess returns in year 1; 7.3% for small caps.
Where to find it: OpenInsider.com — search your ticker, filter to transaction code "P - Purchase" in the past 90 days, count unique insider names. Also check openinsider.com/latest-cluster-buys. Alternative: Finviz.com → ticker → "Insider Trading" tab. Only count open-market purchases (code P). Ignore options exercises, gifts, and 10b5-1 pre-planned trades (check the Form 4 checkbox).

Portfolio & Stock Inputs (Sections 1–3)
1
Black Swan Floor = Cash Reserve × 0.175
$50,000 × 0.175 = $8,750
Reserves 17.5% of cash as untouchable emergency fund for drawdowns exceeding −40%. Historical bear markets average −33% over 406 days; the worst reached −57%. Morgan Stanley (2025) found 54% of individual stocks never return to prior highs — this buffer preserves capital for those events.
2
ATR% = ATR(14) ÷ Current Price × 100
ATR = $3.33, Price = $185 → 3.33 ÷ 185 × 100 = 1.80%
Converts ATR from dollars to a percentage so it is comparable across different-priced stocks. The "ATR%" indicator on TradingView performs this calculation automatically.
📍 TradingView → Indicators → search "ATR%" → reads directly as a percentage
3
Drawdown % = (Recent High − Current Price) ÷ Recent High × 100
High = $210, Price = $185 → (210 − 185) ÷ 210 × 100 = 11.90%
Uses the highest closing price in the past 252 trading days (~1 year). Auto-calculated from Section 3 inputs.
4
Buy Interval = 2 × ATR% × 1.75
ATR% = 1.8% → 2 × 1.8 × 1.75 = 6.3% interval
Calibrates tier spacing to the stock's natural daily volatility. A 5% drop in a utility stock is meaningful; the same move in a high-beta tech name is noise. The 1.75 multiplier prevents triggers on routine daily swings.
5
Tier fires when Drawdown% ≥ Tier# × Interval%
Interval = 6.3%: T1 at −6.3% | T2 at −12.6% | T3 at −18.9% | T4 at −25.2% | T5 at −31.5%
Each tier fires at a cumulative multiple of the interval. If drawdown is 14% and interval is 6.3%: Tier 2 triggered (2 × 6.3 = 12.6% ≤ 14%); Tier 3 not yet (18.9% > 14%).

Volume Ratio = Today's Volume ÷ 20-Day Average Volume
85,000,000 ÷ 37,000,000 = 2.30× → capitulation → +2 pts

9
Z-Score = (Current Price − 20-Day SMA) ÷ 20-Day Standard Deviation
Price = $185, SMA = $196, StdDev = $5.30 → (185 − 196) ÷ 5.30 = −2.08 → +0.75 pts
Note on units: StdDev IS expressed in dollars (same units as price). The division of a dollar value by a dollar value produces a dimensionless (unitless) Z-score. This is correct. Poterba & Summers (1988): transitory price components with StdDev 15–25% account for >50% of monthly return variance, supporting mean-reversion basis for this signal.
⚠ v2 Correction: Weight dramatically reduced for 3–6 month holds. At intermediate horizons (3–12 months), stocks with strongly negative Z-scores have negative momentum that PERSISTS (Jegadeesh & Titman 1993). Mean reversion only reliably dominates beyond 12–18 months (Fama & French 1988). Weight is highest for 1+ year strategies.

⚠ v2 Correction: Threshold raised from 1.5× to 1.65× per SentimenTrader's tested reversal concept. Max raised to 1.0 pts (from 0.5) with graduated scoring. Signal best at 1–4 months; rapidly diminishing beyond that.

Oscillator = 20-Day SMA ÷ 252-Day SMA
20-day SMA = $191.50, 252-day SMA = $196.80 → 191.50 ÷ 196.80 = 0.973 → ≥ 0.95 → +1.0 pt
20 trading days ≈ 4 calendar weeks. 252 trading days ≈ 1 trading year. In Excel: =AVERAGE(last 20 closes) / AVERAGE(last 252 closes).
⚠ v2 Major Correction: Direction INVERTED from previous version. S&P Global (2018) study found that among dip stocks, those with HIGHER oscillator values (maintaining better relative trend) predicted better 240-day forward returns — 49.33% for top half vs 28.40% for all dip stocks. The study used EMA not SMA and ranked by relative percentile, not an absolute 0.95 threshold. Higher oscillator = stock held its trend despite the dip = better candidate.
12
VIX Spike Ratio = Current VIX ÷ VIX 20-Day Moving Average
VIX = 32, 20-day avg VIX = 20 → 32 ÷ 20 = 1.60× → ≥ 1.5× → +0.5 pt bonus
Bansal & Stivers (2025, Financial Management): nonlinear VIX threshold approach "appreciably and consistently outperforms linear VIX approaches." Combined VIX threshold + Baker-Wurgler sentiment explained R²=19% of 6-month returns and R²=29% of 12-month returns. Spike detection identifies when VIX is unusually high relative to its own recent history — more predictive than absolute levels alone.

16
Insider Buy Score = f(Unique Insiders Buying, past 90 days)
3 insiders buy in 90 days = +1.0 pt (cluster buy · peak signal · Strategy B weight 1.5×)
Scoring tiers: 0 insiders = 0 pts · 1 insider = +0.25 pts · 2 insiders = +0.50 pts · 3+ insiders = +1.0 pts (cluster). Key studies: Lakonishok & Lee (2001, RFS): strong-buy insider portfolios earned 4.8% annual excess returns (7.3% for small-caps). Jeng, Metrick & Zeckhauser (2003, REStat): 4-factor alpha of 52 bps/month (~6.4% annualized) for insider purchases. Cohen, Malloy & Pomorski (2012, JOF): opportunistic (non-routine) insiders earned 82 bps/month value-weighted (9.8% annualized). ⚠ Small-cap caveat: Signal is strongest for small-caps (7.3% excess return) and weak for large-caps (1.3%, statistically insignificant per Lakonishok & Lee). Weight accordingly — for mega-cap stocks, treat this as a tiebreaker rather than a primary signal.

Buy Amount Formulas (Section 5)
13
Deploy % = f(Composite Score, Holding Period Strategy)
Strategy B, Score = 6.5 → Deploy = 75% of scaled tier amount
Deployment thresholds are stricter for shorter holding periods (Strategy C) because noise dominates signal at those horizons (Campbell & Shiller 1988 — R² approaches zero at monthly horizons). At 1+ year horizons, fundamental predictability is meaningfully higher so moderate signal confluence already justifies deployment. Grounded in fractional Kelly Criterion theory (MacLean, Thorp & Ziemba 2010).
14
Final Buy Amount = Scaled Tier × Deploy % × Gate Multiplier
Scaled = $3,500 × 75% × 1.0 (above MA) = $2,625
Same, 8% below MA (mult 0.75) → $3,500 × 75% × 0.75 = $1,969
Three multipliers control the output: (1) drawdown depth → tier, (2) composite score → deploy %, (3) MA distance → gate multiplier. All three must align for a full-size purchase.
15
Scaled Tier Amount = Base Amount × (Portfolio Size ÷ 200,000)
Base = $5,500, Portfolio = $100,000 → 5,500 × (100,000 ÷ 200,000) = $2,750
All tier amounts are calibrated to a $200,000 reference portfolio and scale proportionally. A $50K portfolio deploys 25% of base amounts; a $400K portfolio deploys 200%. Consistent risk exposure across all portfolio sizes.
